Scaling observations

  • Prompt Adaptation improves fastest: +0.041 F1 and +0.035 acc from 1 ? 4 shots.
  • All probes beat zero-shot by 10 shots; best margin is Prompt Adaptation (+0.058 acc).
  • CoOp outperforms CE/MLP at every shot, but trails Prompt Adaptation.
  • Zero-shot is flat (prompt-only) and remains a strong 1-shot baseline.

Practical implications

  • Quick win: 4 shots already beats zero-shot for all methods.
  • Best 10-shot pick: Prompt Adaptation (0.4701 acc / 0.4469 F1).
  • If compute-limited: CE probe is simplest and stable; MLP is fastest to train.
  • Label-scarce: Zero-shot is viable; CoOp at 1 shot offers the biggest jump over zero-shot.

Computational cost

Efficiency analysis

Training time, memory, and inference speed trade-offs.

Training efficiency

  • Fastest (trained): Few-shot MLP probe — 0.16 h
  • Slowest: Few-shot CE probe — 0.38 h (≈2.3× slower than MLP)
  • Prompt Adaptation: ≈ 0.30 h for best accuracy
  • Zero-shot: no training time

Inference deployment

  • Fastest inference: Zero-shot CLIP at 7.35 ms/img (136 img/s).
  • Best accuracy–latency trade-off: Prompt Adaptation at 7.49 ms/img (133 img/s).
  • Slowest among compared models: MLP probe at 8.46 ms/img (118 img/s).
  • Latency spread is narrow, so accuracy/robustness should drive deployment decisions.

Insights

Main findings

Key takeaways and practical implications.

Overall performance ranking

  • Best accuracy: Few-shot Prompt Adaptation — 47.3% test acc / 44.96% macro-F1 with 10 shots
  • Best trade-off: Prompt Adaptation keeps latency at 7.49 ms/img (second-fastest) while topping accuracy
  • Surprising finding: Linear MLP probe is faster to train (0.16 h) but does not beat CE probe on accuracy
  • Baseline gap: +5.21 acc pts and +6.08 macro-F1 pts over zero-shot CLIP
Finding 1: Prompt tuning beats probes with similar latency

Prompt Adaptation delivers the best accuracy while staying within 0.14 ms/img of zero-shot inference — an attractive trade-off for production.

Finding 2: CE probe is the steadier baseline

Despite faster training, the MLP probe lags behind the simpler CE head on both Acc and macro-F1, suggesting the linear head remains the safer default.

Finding 3: Zero-shot remains competitive for latency-only scenarios

At 7.35 ms/img and no training cost, zero-shot CLIP is still viable when labeling is impossible, giving only ~5 accuracy points away to the best few-shot setup.

Recommendations for practitioners

  • Maximum accuracy: Few-shot Prompt Adaptation with 10 shots per class.
  • Quick prototyping: Zero-shot CLIP (no training, 42.05% test accuracy).
  • Resource-constrained training: Few-shot MLP probe is fastest to train; CE probe is steadier on accuracy.
  • Production deployment: Few-shot Prompt Adaptation — best accuracy with near zero-shot latency.
