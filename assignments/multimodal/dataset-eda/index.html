Foundation

Dataset selection & motivation

Why this dataset is suitable for multimodal classification.

Dataset: N24News (NYTimes)

Multimodal news dataset (images + text metadata) with 61,218 samples across 24 sections; used here as a 24-class image classification benchmark.

  • Each sample: news image, section label, headline, abstract, caption, article URL, image URL, image ID.
  • Primary modality for training: image; prompts use class names.
  • Source: New York Times articles; processed split matches counts in stats.

Why this dataset?

Real-world, multi-domain news where images often hint at the section label, but overlaps force fine-grained discrimination.

  • Applications: news search, recommendations, editorial curation.
  • Challenge: confusing lookalikes (Economy vs Opinion vs Your Money).
  • Multimodal headroom: headlines/abstracts for fusion or prompt design.
  • Licensing: NYT-derived; processed release complies with dataset terms.

Representation space

Embedding projections

Test-set embeddings (5,000 samples, 512-d). Visualized with UMAP, t-SNE, PCA; cosine similarity mined for near-neighbor classes.

Projection setup

  • Embeddings shape: 5,000 × 512; skipped batches: 0.
  • Labels shape: 5,000.
  • Dim. reduction: PCA → t-SNE/UMAP on 50 PC preload.
First 10 PC variance ratios: 0.0552, 0.0470, 0.0314, 0.0306, 0.0272, 0.0224, 0.0211, 0.0186, 0.0176, 0.0174.
Cumulative: PC2 = 0.1022, PC10 = 0.2885. PCs for 90/95/99%: 235 / 309 / 427.

Interpretation tips

  • UMAP/t-SNE: cluster tightness ↔ semantic coherence; mixed clusters may signal label noise.
  • PCA scatter: linear separability in top components; elongated shapes suggest correlated features.
  • Variance curve: elbow helps choose projection depth before downstream tasks.

Important EDA findings

Key observations and implications for model design.

🔍 Dataset characteristics

  • Class balance: Describe whether dataset is balanced or imbalanced. Implications for loss functions and metrics.
  • Modality importance: Which modality (image vs text) appears more informative? Any visual-text conflicts?
  • Data quality: Are there noisy labels, low-quality images, or malformed text? What preprocessing is needed?
  • Domain variation: How much visual and textual diversity is there within classes?
  • Temporal or sequential patterns: Any temporal ordering or dependencies between samples?

  • Modality-specific architectures
