Few-shot approach

Adapter-based Tuning for Few-shot Multimodal Classification

Lightweight adapter blocks are inserted into a frozen pretrained backbone to adapt the model with only a few labeled samples. This preserves most pretrained weights while allowing task-specific conditioning through small bottleneck modules.

Method

Adapter-based few-shot adaptation

Insert trainable bottleneck modules while freezing the main vision-language backbone.

Key ideas

  • Freeze backbone encoders; train only adapter layers and classifier head.
  • Use small bottleneck dimension (e.g., 64-256) to control parameter count.
  • Optionally share adapters across layers or modalities to reduce size.
  • Retain pretrained representations while enabling task-specific conditioning.

Why adapters?

  • Parameter efficient compared to full fine-tuning.
  • Faster training; lower memory footprint.
  • Easy to ablate adapter depth/width.
  • Compatible with linear probe as a fallback.

Design choices

  • Where to insert: vision encoder, text encoder, or both.
  • Bottleneck size and activation (e.g., GELU).
  • Dropout on adapter outputs.
  • Initialization strategy (e.g., near-zero init).

Takeaways

Advantages and risks

Summarize when adapters are preferable and where they may underperform.

Use adapters when

  • GPU memory is limited
  • Rapid iteration is needed
  • Domain shift is moderate
  • You need parameter-efficient fine-tuning

Consider alternatives if

  • Maximum accuracy is required
  • Domain shift is extreme
  • Latency budget is tight
  • You can afford full fine-tuning
