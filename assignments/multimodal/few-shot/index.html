🔧 Few-shot Learning · Minimal labels

Few-shot Multimodal Learning

Few-shot learning adapts pretrained models to a specific task using only a small number of labeled examples (typically 16-128 samples). We compare multiple adaptation strategies to find the best balance between performance, computational cost, and ease of implementation.

Concept

What is few-shot learning?

Understanding the few-shot paradigm and why it matters.

Few-shot vs. other paradigms

  • Zero-shot: No labeled data, prompt-based
  • Few-shot: 16-128 labeled examples, adaptation
  • Fine-tuning: 1000+ labeled examples, full training
  • Semi-supervised: Mix of labeled and unlabeled

Practical scenarios

  • New product category (e-commerce)
  • Emerging disease detection (medical)
  • Rare events (security/anomaly)
  • Domain adaptation challenges
  • Resource-constrained edge AI
