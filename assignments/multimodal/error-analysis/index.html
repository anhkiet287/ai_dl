Error Analysis & Failure Cases

Error analysis shows where multimodal classifiers still break. Most mistakes cluster in semantically adjacent sections and in samples with weak or cross-domain cues. Prompt Adaptation leads, but persistent confusion families remain.

Best analyzed method

Few-shot Prompt Adaptation — anchor method for all comparisons.

Persistent confusion family

Entertainment/editorial: Movies ↔ Television, Music → Theater.

Hardest recurring classes

Style, Media, Your Money, Well, Economy.

Main lesson

Semantic overlap matters more than raw confidence; many wrong predictions are high-confidence.

Multimodal perspective

Modality-specific failure modes

Image, text, and cross-modal ambiguity each drive distinct errors.

Image-dominant mistakes

Travel → Food (restaurant imagery), Technology → Automobiles (cars in frame), Style → Fashion (runway visuals). Images override textual context.

Text-dominant mistakes

Your Money → Education (financial-aid wording) and Music → Theater (headline mentions performance) despite mixed visuals.

Cross-modal ambiguity

Media → Technology: both modalities reference platforms; taxonomy itself is fuzzy. High-confidence errors signal overlapping label space, not just model failure.

Method comparison

Method-specific error patterns

How the three methods differ in strengths and failure modes.

Zero-shot CLIP

  • Strength: Interpretable saliency, no training.
  • Failures: Travel → Real Estate, Music → Theater; high confidence even when wrong.
  • Pattern: Relies on coarse scene cues; lacks fine-grained editorial wording.
  • Implication: Needs prompt enrichment for macro/business vs lifestyle distinctions.

Few-shot Prompt Adaptation

  • Strength: Best overall balance; major gains for Global Business, Television, Technology.
  • Failures: Movies ↔ Television, Fashion ↔ Style, Media drift into Technology/Opinion.
  • Pattern: Improves weak classes but still struggles with overlapping lifestyle/finance labels.
  • Implication: Add class-specific negative prompts and calibrate confidence.

Few-shot CoOp

  • Strength: Strong gains on Global Business and Books.
  • Failures: Well collapses (F1 0.0552); Health → Science and Style → Fashion dominate errors.
  • Pattern: More brittle to class imbalance; prompt tokens overfit to dominant visuals.
  • Implication: Requires re-weighting or balanced episodes for lifestyle/health classes.
Takeaways

Practical lessons learned

Actionable guidance derived from the failures and patterns.

Semantic neighbors dominate

Remaining errors cluster in adjacent editorial categories; outright off-topic mistakes are rare.

Confidence is not correctness

Many wrong predictions exceed 0.95 probability; calibration and selective prediction are needed.

Few-shot helps broad labels

Largest gains are for broad business/editorial classes; visually distinctive classes see modest change.

Label space ambiguity

Some boundaries (Media vs Technology, Style vs Fashion) are inherently fuzzy in news taxonomy.

Recommendations · Data curation

  • Add more disambiguating samples for Style, Media, Your Money, Well, Economy.
  • Capture mixed-modality cases (e.g., concerts vs theater) with explicit labels.
  • Audit Media/Technology/Opinion overlaps; relabel or add secondary tags.

Recommendations · Prompt / label engineering

  • Enrich prompts with format cues (film vs series), financial scope (macro vs personal).
  • Add class-specific negatives to reduce cross-domain drift.
  • Use calibration or temperature scaling to down-weight overconfident ambiguous cases.

Recommendations · Modeling

  • Class-weight or re-sample for Well and Your Money to counter imbalance.
  • Contrastive training with hard negatives for Movies/Television and Style/Fashion.
  • Late-fusion or text-aware prompts for Media vs Technology boundary cases.
