## Dataset: ImageNet

### 1. Dataset Description

**ImageNet** is one of the largest and most widely used datasets in **computer vision**, especially for the **image classification** task. It is constructed based on the structure of **WordNet**, where each class corresponds to a semantic concept (synset).

The full ImageNet dataset contains:

* Over **14 million images**
* More than **20,000 classes**

In practice, the most commonly used subset is **ImageNet-1K**, which includes:

* **1.28 million training images**
* **50,000 validation images**
* **1000 classes**

The dataset is highly diverse in terms of:

* object appearance
* viewpoints
* lighting conditions
* background complexity

This diversity enables models to learn robust and generalizable visual representations.

### 3. Task

In this project, ImageNet is used for the following task:

> **Image Classification**

Specifically:

* **Input**: an image
* **Output**: a label among **1000 classes**
* **Objective**: correctly predict the class of the input image

### 5. Why We Choose ImageNet

We choose ImageNet for the classification task for the following reasons:

* **Standard benchmark**
  ImageNet is a widely accepted benchmark in computer vision, allowing fair comparison with prior work.

* **Large-scale dataset**
  Its massive size supports training deep learning models effectively and improves performance.

* **High diversity**
  The dataset contains a wide variety of classes and visual conditions, which enhances generalization ability.

* **Compatibility with modern architectures**
  Models such as Vision Transformers (ViT) are commonly evaluated on ImageNet, making it a suitable choice.

* **Availability of pretrained models**
  Many pretrained models are available on ImageNet, enabling efficient transfer learning and fine-tuning.
